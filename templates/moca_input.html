Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MOCA)

Visuospatial/Executive

The psychometrician should present the patient with five tasks to assess visuospatial and executive functions.

1. Draw a line to connect alternating letters and numbers (e.g., 1-A, 2-B, etc.)

2. Copy the cube shown below

3. Draw a clock (10 past 11) with the contour, numbers, and hands correctly placed

4. Place the numbers correctly on the clock face

5. Set the clock hands to 10 past 11

Naming

The psychometrician should show the patient pictures of three animals and ask them to name each one.

6. Name the animal shown below (Lion)

7. Name the animal shown below (Rhinoceros)

8. Name the animal shown below (Camel)

Memory

The psychometrician should ask the patient to recall the following words immediately: 'face, velvet, church, daisy, red'. Assess whether the patient can recall each word correctly.

9. Immediate recall of 'face'

10. Immediate recall of 'velvet'

11. Immediate recall of 'church'

12. Immediate recall of 'daisy'

13. Immediate recall of 'red'

Delayed Recall

The psychometrician should ask the patient to recall the words 'face, velvet, church, daisy, red' after a delay.

14. Delayed recall of 'face'

15. Delayed recall of 'velvet'

16. Delayed recall of 'church'

17. Delayed recall of 'daisy'

18. Delayed recall of 'red'

Attention

The psychometrician should assess the patient's attention by having them repeat sequences of numbers.

19. The psychometrician reads '2 1 8 5 4'. The patient must repeat the numbers in the same forward order.

20. The psychometrician reads '7 4 2'. The patient must repeat the numbers in reverse order.

The next task is to assess the patient's attention by reading a list of letters: FBACMNAAJKLBAFAKDEAAAJAMOFAAB. The patient must tap with their hand each time they hear the letter 'A'.

21. Tap for each letter 'A' in the sequence

The psychometrician should assess the patient's attention by asking them to perform a serial subtraction of 7, starting at 100. The patient should continue subtracting until reaching 65.

22. Subtract 7 from 100 (Answer: 93)

23. Subtract 7 from 93 (Answer: 86)

24. Subtract 7 from 86 (Answer: 79)

25. Subtract 7 from 79 (Answer: 72)

26. Subtract 7 from 72 (Answer: 65)

Language

The psychometrician should ask the patient to repeat the following sentences:

27. Repeat: "I only know that John is the one to help today."

29. Name more than 11 words that begin with the letter 'F'

Abstraction

The psychometrician should ask the patient to find the similarity between two words.

30. Train - Bicycle

31. Watch - Ruler

Orientation

The psychometrician should ask the patient questions about the current date, month, year, day, place, and city to assess their orientation.

37. What is the current date?

38. What is the current month?

39. What is the current year?

40. What is the current day of the week?

41. Where are we right now (place)?

42. What city are we in?
